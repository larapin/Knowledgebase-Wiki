# Tooling and cutting parameters

## Planing fiberglass
- Machining thickness: .050 inch
- Feed speed: 45 IPM
- Spindle speed: 15000 RPM

## Chip load formulas
Helpful formulas:
- Chip load = Feed rate / (RPM * number of cutting edges)
- Feed rate = RPM * cutting edges * chip load
- RPM = Feed rate / (cutting edges * chip load)

## Heat buildup considerations
- Plunging creates a lot of heat and can shorten tool life
- Corner deceleration also builds heat
- Short segments at feed speed reduce chip load and can increase heat

## Finishing pass guidance
- Ball nose finishing step-over should be approximately 6% of cutter diameter for a good finish

## Typical tooling examples
- 1/2 inch Vortex compression tool: about 1200 IPM at 18000 RPM in plywood
- 3/8 inch Vortex compression tool: about 300 to 600 IPM at 18000 RPM
- 1/4 inch Vortex compression tool: about 250 to 400 IPM at 18000 RPM
- 5 mm drill/end mill: about 150 IPM, 12000 to 18000 RPM

## Material notes
- Baltic birch: around 120 IPM
- Balsa: around 150 IPM
- EVA foam: often used for foot orthotics

## Shaper and molding heads
Shaper heads may be used if:
- correct arbor is used
- fastening method prevents loosening
- diameter does not exceed 2 inch specification for auto tool changer machines
- head does not extend more than 2.5 inches from the collet
- the arbor and cutter are balanced and rated for the RPM being used

## Vacuum and material notes
- Diamond tooling is used for granite
- Cooling flood is useful for diamond tooling in hard materials
